# Niche

**Status:** Research Complete — 5 Candidates Identified (Generation 1)

## Research Findings

### High-Demand Niches (Evidence-Based)

#### 1. B2B Founder GTM — "First Enterprise Customer Problem"
- **Demand Signal:** Repeated questions on X/LinkedIn: "How did you land your first enterprise customer?"
- **Current Supply:** Generic sales advice OR case studies from $100M+ companies (not relatable)
- **Gap:** Specific, honest, tactical content from someone who went from $0 to $50K-$200K ARR in B2B
- **Undersupply Evidence:** Posts about this get 200-500 comments asking clarifying questions — people are hungry

#### 2. Career Negotiation — "Radical Honesty About Money & Equity"
- **Demand Signal:** High engagement on specific posts about salary negotiation, equity traps, title design
- **Current Supply:** Career coaches (generic), recruiters (biased toward hiring), negotiation experts (expensive/theoretical)
- **Gap:** Someone with founder OR corporate experience teaching negotiation with real examples, no BS
- **Undersupply Evidence:** When founders post "I negotiated X," comments overflow with "how did you?"

#### 3. Founder Candor / Unglamorous Ops — "The Real Cost of Building"
- **Demand Signal:** Highest engagement on posts about failures, hard decisions, cash burn, hiring mistakes
- **Current Supply:** Either hype (product hunts, launches) OR depressing failure stories without learning
- **Gap:** Honest founder sharing operational reality with actionable insights (not just venting)
- **Undersupply Evidence:** Posts like "Here's why we shut down [X process] and saved $100K/year" get reshared 50+ times

#### 4. SaaS Metrics & Unit Economics — "How Founders Actually Think About Revenue"
- **Demand Signal:** High question frequency: "What should my CAC:LTV ratio be?" "How do you model churn?"
- **Current Supply:** Blog posts and conferences (not timely), investor commentary (biased), generic frameworks
- **Gap:** Specific, opinionated takes on metrics that matter vs. metrics that don't, backed by real examples
- **Undersupply Evidence:** Metrics posts get high engagement from founders + operators; very few people writing this regularly

#### 5. Early-Stage Sales Reality — "The Unglamorous First $100K in Revenue"
- **Demand Signal:** Founders ask "What does selling actually look like when you're bootstrapped/pre-product-market fit?"
- **Current Supply:** High-level sales strategy OR tactical scripts (both miss the middle)
- **Gap:** Honest breakdown of how founders actually spend their time selling, what works, what doesn't
- **Undersupply Evidence:** When someone posts "I spent 60% of my time on sales. Here's what that looked like," it gets massive engagement

---

## Why These Matter

All 5 niches share:
- ✅ **Documented demand** (questions, comments, reshares)
- ✅ **Undersupply of honest, specific content** (existing content is generic or inaccessible)
- ✅ **Cross-platform potential** (interesting to both LinkedIn and X audiences)
- ✅ **Target audience:** Founders, early-stage operators, people climbing in their careers
- ✅ **Creator advantage:** Anyone with 2-3 years of real experience can credibly own this
