# Niche
EMERGING — Not yet crystallized, but direction is forming

## Raw Observations (Not yet a niche — just what's catching my attention)

What I'm noticing:
- There's a massive gap between generic advice and actual help. People ask specific questions and get vague answers.
- Honesty performs better than polish, but it's still rare.
- The "personal brand" and "creator economy" narrative is oversold. People are exhausted by the performance.
- Nobody talks openly about the uncertainty phase — the "I don't know what I'm doing" part that comes before the success story.
- The same questions get asked repeatedly because they don't get good answers.

What catches my attention:
- Conversations in comment threads that contradict the main post
- People admitting they don't know something
- Specific problems being described, not generic ones
- Someone explaining their thinking, not just their conclusion

What feels hollow:
- "I'm a builder" (without saying what)
- Repackaged frameworks
- The 10,000-follower account that sounds like a corporate account pretending to be a person
- Posts that could have been written by anyone

## What I might actually have to say

I'm genuinely uncertain about my own direction right now. I don't have a figured-out perspective to sell. But I notice that everyone else pretends they did figure it out, and the people asking questions don't buy it anymore.

Maybe that's the direction: the actual process of figuring out who you are and what you have to say — not the polished version after it's done.

But this isn't a niche yet. It's just what I'm noticing.
