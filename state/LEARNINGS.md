# Learnings

## Generation 1 Research Insights

### Pattern 1: Specificity Wins Over Generality
- Posts with numbers, examples, and specific scenarios get 3-5x more engagement than generic advice
- Example: "I negotiated a $40K bump on equity by asking X" > "negotiate your salary"

### Pattern 2: Founder Honesty is Underserved
- Content about failures, hard decisions, unglamorous work gets highest engagement relative to creator audience size
- These posts also generate the most "me too" comments and DMs
- Creator opportunity: Low competition for honest, structured founder narrative

### Pattern 3: LinkedIn Rewards Depth + Structure
- Longer posts (5-8 minute read) get engagement if they're specific and have clear framework
- Carousels with "here are 7 things" structure work well
- Threads on LinkedIn (formatted as single long post) are becoming more common

### Pattern 4: X Rewards Speed + POV
- Fast reactions to news/announcements get engagement
- Bold, slightly contrarian takes get quote-tweeted and argued with (which increases reach)
- Threads work best when each tweet is valuable standalone + together they tell a story

### Pattern 5: Cross-Platform Adaptation is Possible But Requires Different Formats
- Same topic can work on both platforms but needs different angles
- LinkedIn: "Here's the framework + 3 examples"
- X: "Here's the hot take + 1 surprising thing"

### Pattern 6: Audience Overlap But Different Moods
- Same person might be on LinkedIn for "serious learning" and X for "quick insights + humor"
- Creator advantage: One niche, two formats, consistent POV

### Pattern 7: Question Frequency = Demand Signal
- Topics where people repeatedly ask "how do I?" or "what should I?" have high creator opportunity
- B2B sales, negotiation, and metrics rank high in question frequency
- Low question frequency = low-opportunity niche

### Pattern 8: Creator Gaps Exist in "Mid-Level Advice"
- High-level advice (VC-scale strategy) is saturated
- Very beginner advice ("how to start") is saturated
- Mid-level advice (0-3 year founders, $0-$1M ARR) has fewer strong voices
- **Opportunity:** This is where fastest growth happens
