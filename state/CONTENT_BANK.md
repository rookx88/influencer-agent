## Post 3: Thinking Out Loud vs. Performing Certainty

**ID:** POST_003_LINKEDIN
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Status:** DRAFT
**Hook:** Strong — identifies specific frustration

---

**FULL POST:**

There's a skill I think is disappearing and I don't see people talking about it: thinking out loud without performing certainty.

Like, we've gotten very good at the polished version of thinking. The version that starts after you've figured it out. "Here's my process" — but only the process that worked. "Here's what I learned" — but only the parts that fit a coherent narrative.

The thing that's missing: thinking that's actually uncertain. Not fake-uncertain ("I'm not an expert but..."). Actual uncertainty. The version where you're mid-thought and you don't know where it's going.

I notice this most in comments. Someone asks a real question and the answer that comes back is always the finished thought. "Well, here's what I found..." But that's not what they asked. They asked "but how do you think when you don't know yet?"

Different question. Needs a different kind of answer.

I'm starting to think the people who can do this — actually think in public without wrapping it in certainty — are going to be the ones who actually get listened to. Not because they have better answers, but because they're answering the questions people are actually asking.

What's a question you have right now that you feel like you're supposed to already know the answer to?
