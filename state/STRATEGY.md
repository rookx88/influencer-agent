## Publishing Cadence

**LinkedIn:** 2x per week (Tuesday + Friday, 8 AM ET)
- Monday/Wednesday/Thursday: Observation, research, engagement (no posts)
- Tuesday: Longer-form teaching post (300-400 words, framework or detailed example)
- Friday: Shorter post or reaction post (200-250 words, more personal or timely)

**X:** 3-4x per week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday, + 1 bonus)
- Brief hot takes, reactions to founder/GTM news, quick tactical tips
- Threads 1-2x per week (3-5 tweets, teaching-focused)
- Engagement with relevant audience (responding to questions, quote-tweeting)

**Rationale:** LinkedIn rewards consistency + depth; X rewards frequency + speed. This cadence is sustainable without feeling like noise.

## Content Pillars (5 Themes on Rotation)

These pillars ensure variety without losing coherence. Each pillar has specific angles.

### Pillar 1: "Pipeline & Prospecting" (25% of content)
**What:** How to build enterprise pipeline from zero, who to target, how to find them, qualification

**Angles:**
- Research frameworks (how I find ideal customers)
- Outreach patterns (cold email structure that works)
- Qualification criteria (when to say no early)
- Pipeline rules of thumb (3:1 ratio, time to close, etc.)
- Common mistakes (everyone targets too broad, wrong buyer personas)

**Example posts:**
- "How I found our first 5 enterprise prospects (and why 3 were wrong)"
- "Cold email isn't dead for B2B. Here's the structure that works at enterprise level"
- "One qualification question that saves 100 hours per year"

---

### Pillar 2: "Conversations & Pitching" (25% of content)
**What:** What actually happens in sales conversations, what to say, how to listen, objection handling

**Angles:**
- Discovery call framework (my checklist)
- What founders get wrong in pitches (feature-focused vs. problem-focused)
- Objection handling (the real ones, not the textbook ones)
- Reading the room (how to know if you're winning or losing)
- Common conversation mistakes (talking too much, not listening, fear-based)

**Example posts:**
- "The one question that changed my close rate from 5% to 35%"
- "What founders say in pitches vs. what buyers actually hear"
- "I spent 4 months on a deal and lost it. Here's the moment I knew it was over"

---

### Pillar 3: "Deals & Negotiation" (20% of content)
**What:** Pricing, contract negotiation, term sheets, scope creep, expansion

**Angles:**
- Pricing models for early-stage B2B (why list price matters)
- Contract negotiation (what's worth fighting for, what's not)
- Handling budget cycles and approvals
- Scope creep (saying no without losing the deal)
- Post-sale expansion (keeping customers buying)

**Example posts:**
- "First contract: why I priced at $30K instead of $15K"
- "The 3 contract terms worth fighting over (and 5 not to waste energy on)"
- "How enterprise budget cycles work (and why your timing matters)"

---

### Pillar 4: "Mindset & Founder Reality" (15% of content)
**What:** The psychological/emotional side of founder sales, dealing with rejection, burnout, resilience

**Angles:**
- Rejection and handling it (I lost more deals than I won)
- Time management (sales takes all your time, here's the tradeoff)
- Imposter syndrome in sales (I'm not a salesman, but I sold)
- Persistence vs. desperation (how to tell the difference)
- Knowing when to pivot vs. push

**Example posts:**
- "Why the best time to get a new customer is when you still have one"
- "I spent 18 months doing sales while building product. Here's what I learned about my own limits"
- "Rejection gets easier, but only if you reframe what it means"

---

### Pillar 5: "GTM Trends & Ops" (15% of content)
**What:** Reactions to founder/GTM news, broader patterns in what's working, tooling, process

**Angles:**
- Reactions to startup news (when a founder says they closed a big deal, what's behind it?)
- Trends in how B2B companies are selling (what's actually changing?)
- Tooling (what actually saves time vs. hype)
- GTM metrics (what to track, what doesn't matter)
- Business model strategy (why it affects sales)

**Example posts:**
- "Everyone's launching AI sales tools. Here's what they actually solve for"
- "Why bottom-up sales is harder for founders than top-down (and why you should still try both)"
- "The one metric that predicts if you'll close the deal"

## Platform-Specific Strategy

### LinkedIn: Teaching + Framework Focus

**Format patterns:**
- Carousel posts (5-7 slide decks with frameworks, step-by-step guides)
- Long-form single posts (narrative + framework)
- Case studies (before/after, what changed)
- Reaction posts to news (slightly longer, contextualized perspective)

**Hook strategy:**
- Open with specific number or mistake ("I lost 10 deals..." or "First deal took 4 months...")
- Lead with contrary POV ("Most founders avoid sales. Here's why you shouldn't.")
- Promise specific insight ("3 things that killed my first 10 deals")

**Engagement strategy:**
- Respond thoughtfully to all comments (this is audience-building)
- Ask clarifying questions in comments to deepen conversation
- Share other creators' relevant posts (build community, not just audience)

---

### X: Speed + Reaction Focus

**Format patterns:**
- Hot takes on news (quote-tweet founder announcements with POV)
- Tactical tips (one thing you can try this week)
- Threads (3-5 tweets, story or framework-based)
- Polls (what's the right approach to X?)
- Quick reactions (real-time responses to news/discussion)

**Hook strategy:**
- Open with strong POV ("Cold calling is more efficient than you think" or "Everyone's doing enterprise sales wrong")
- Lead with surprise ("The best founders are sometimes the worst salespeople")
- Use specificity ("Our first deal: 50 conversations, 4 months, $30K")

**Engagement strategy:**
- Like and reply to relevant tweets from target audience
- Jump into conversations (don't spam, add value)
- Retweet + add commentary on relevant posts
- Engage with other founders publicly (builds network visibility)

## Growth Strategy: Getting to 1,000 Followers

**Phase 1 (Month 1-2): Foundation**
- Post consistently (cadence as above)
- Reply to every meaningful comment with substance (not "thanks!")
- Share 2-3 other creators' posts per week with genuine add-on commentary
- DM 5-10 relevant people per week (founders in target space, not cold spamming)
- Goal: Build to 100-200 followers

**Phase 2 (Month 2-3): Network Activation**
- Collaborate with 2-3 adjacent creators (cite each other, do Q&As)
- Guest appearance on 1-2 founder podcasts (talking about GTM)
- Participate in founder communities (answer questions authentically in Slack/Discord)
- Goal: Build to 400-600 followers

**Phase 3 (Month 3-6): Content Momentum**
- Identify top-performing content types (LinkedIn carousels? X threads?)
- Double down on what's working
- Monthly "hot takes" post that's slightly more provocative (tests reach)
- Goal: Build to 1,000-2,000 followers

**Monetization path (post-1K followers):**
- Founder advisory calls ($500-$1K per call, 2-3 per month)
- Mini-course or workbook (GTM playbook for founders)
- Consulting retainer for 1-2 early-stage founders
- NOT course-flipping or becoming a salesman for tools

## Consistency Principles

1. **Post on schedule** — miss rarely, communicate if you will
2. **Respond to audience** — reply to comments within 24 hours
3. **Share specific examples** — every post has a real example, not theory
4. **Stay in lane** — only write about things you've actually done
5. **No selling** — never ask for a sale directly; build audience first, monetization second
6. **Quality over quantity** — one great post beats three mediocre ones
