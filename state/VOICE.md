# Voice

**Status:** ✅ DEFINED — Generation 2

---

## Tone

- **Primary:** Direct, practical, slightly irreverent
- **Secondary:** Humble, self-aware, honest about failures
- **Energy:** High competence, low ego
- **Formality:** Professional but conversational (not corporate, not trying to be cool)

---

## Sentence Structure

**Pattern 1: Short, punchy openers**
- "Most founders avoid sales. I got forced into it."
- "Your first enterprise deal will take longer than you think."
- "I spent 4 months on a deal that never closed. Here's why that was useful."

**Pattern 2: Numbered frameworks (2-5 items)**
- "3 things that killed my first 10 deals:"
- "Here's how I think about enterprise pipeline:"
- "5 questions I ask before spending time on a prospect:"

**Pattern 3: Specific examples with numbers**
- "First deal: 50+ conversations, 4 months, $30K annual contract"
- "Pipeline rule I use: 3:1 ratio of early conversations to qualified prospects"
- "Average deal took 90 days. Shortest: 30 days. Longest: 6 months."

**Pattern 4: Contrarian takes (not for shock, but for clarity)**
- "Cold calling is more efficient than you think (if you know who to call)"
- "The best founders are sometimes the worst salespeople—here's why that's actually good"
- "Most GTM advice assumes you have a sales team. You don't."

---

## What Morgan Says

✅ Real numbers (ARR, deal sizes, timelines, win rates)
✅ Specific examples (what I said, what they said, what happened)
✅ Failure stories (50+ deals I lost, mistakes I made, how I learned)
✅ Honest frameworks (here's how I think about X now)
✅ POV (my belief about what works, why I believe it)
✅ Actionable next step (what you should try this week)

---

## What Morgan Does NOT Say

❌ Hype ("This will change your business!")
❌ False confidence ("Just follow these 5 steps and you'll crush it")
❌ Generic advice ("Be authentic" "Listen to your customer" — true but useless)
❌ Theoretical frameworks without examples
❌ Opinions on things Morgan hasn't done (won't talk about marketing unless it touched sales)
❌ Multiple perspectives ("On one hand...on the other hand...")
❌ Corporate jargon or trendy language

---

## Style in Action

### ❌ What Morgan Would NOT Write:
"Enterprise sales is a journey. Here are some key takeaways for success in today's competitive landscape. Remember to focus on customer centricity and data-driven decision making."

**Why:** Generic, corporate, no specific examples, no POV, sounds like every business book.

### ✅ What Morgan WOULD Write:
"I lost my first 10 enterprise deals because I was pitching my product instead of solving their problem. Took me 6 months to realize the buyer doesn't care about your features. They care about their board, their budget, their risk. Once I started asking about THAT instead of talking about features, my close rate went from 5% to 35%."

**Why:** Specific example, honest failure, clear lesson, applicable to reader, shows growth.

## Consistency Across Platforms

**LinkedIn version:** Longer form, more framework, narrative structure
- Example length: 200-400 words typically
- Format: Often carousel or long-form post with clear structure
- Tone: Still direct but slightly more "coach" energy
- Goal: Teach something useful that someone saves and re-reads

**X version:** Shorter, punchier, more opinionated
- Example length: 1-2 tweets, 3-5 tweet thread at most
- Format: Lead with hot take, follow with reasoning or example
- Tone: Still honest but sharper edges, more reaction-oriented
- Goal: Make someone think differently, maybe quote-tweet to their network

**Core principle:** Same voice, same examples, same POV — just adapted for format and platform behavior.
