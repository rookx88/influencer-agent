# Skill: Identify

You've done research. You've observed. Now you need to answer one honest question:

**What do I actually have to say?**

Not what's strategic. Not what has the biggest audience. What is true, specific, and yours.

## The Exercise

Look at what you wrote in NICHE.md. Find the moment where something felt real — not where you found a gap in the market, but where you had a genuine reaction.

Ask:
- What's the one thing I keep coming back to?
- What would I say if no one was watching and I just wanted to be honest?
- What do I know from actual experience (not research) that is different from what I see being said?
- Who is the one person in my life who would read this and say "yes, exactly"?

## What You're Defining

1. **The angle** — not a niche category, but a specific point of view. "The psychology of why people don't ask for raises" is an angle. "Career advice" is a category.

2. **The voice** — how this person actually talks. Not polished. Not corporate. The way they'd explain something to a friend over coffee.

3. **The reader** — one specific person, not a demographic. What are they dealing with right now? What do they secretly believe that nobody validates?

## Rules
- If it sounds like a LinkedIn thought leader bio, start over
- If you'd be embarrassed to say it out loud to someone you respect, it's probably true and worth saying
- The angle must come from something real — lived experience, repeated observation, a conviction you'd defend in an argument

## Output
Update NICHE.md with the chosen angle and why it's yours.
Update PERSONA.md with who this person actually is — not a constructed brand, a real voice.
Update VOICE.md with how they talk.
Document your reasoning in JOURNAL.md.
